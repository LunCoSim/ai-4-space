# Using Crypto Wallets: A Beginner's Guide

## What are Crypto Wallets?

Cryptocurrency wallets are applications that allow you to interact with blockchain applications (dApps) directly from your web browser. They act as a bridge between you and the blockchain, enabling you to:

- Store and manage cryptocurrency
- Sign transactions securely
- Interact with decentralized applications
- Manage multiple blockchain networks

### What is a Seed Phrase?

A **seed phrase** (also called a recovery phrase, secret phrase, or mnemonic phrase) is a series of **12 or 24 random words** that serves as the master key to your cryptocurrency wallet.

**Example format:**
```
witch collapse practice feed shame open despair creek road again ice least
```

### Why is it Important?

Your seed phrase is **THE MOST IMPORTANT** piece of information for your wallet:

âœ… **Recovery**: It's the **only way** to recover your wallet if you lose access  
âœ… **Permanent**: Once generated, it cannot be changed  
âœ… **No Customer Support**: There is **no "forgot password"** option - if you lose it, your funds are **gone forever**

- **Verify you wrote it correctly** by checking each word
- **Keep it offline** - never store it digitally
- **Test your backup** by restoring your wallet on a different device

## Basic Wallet Operations

### Viewing Your Wallet Address

Your wallet address is your public identifier (like an email address for crypto):

- Click on your account name at the top
- Click the address or "Copy" icon
- It looks like: `0x742d35Cc6634C0532925a3b844Bc9e7595f0bEb`

**Safe to share**: Your wallet address is public and safe to share for receiving funds.

### Receiving Cryptocurrency

1. Click "Receive" or copy your wallet address
2. Share this address with the sender
3. Wait for the transaction to confirm (usually 30 seconds to a few minutes)

### Sending Cryptocurrency

1. Click "Send"
2. Enter the recipient's wallet address
3. Enter the amount to send
4. Review the **gas fee** (transaction cost)
5. Click "Confirm"
6. Wait for confirmation

âš ï¸ **Always double-check the recipient address** - transactions cannot be reversed!

### Switching Networks

MetaMask and Rabby support multiple blockchain networks:

**MetaMask:**
- Click the network dropdown at the top
- Select your desired network (Ethereum, Polygon, BSC, etc.)

**Rabby:**
- Automatically detects the network needed for each dApp
- You can manually switch in settings if needed

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### "Insufficient Funds for Gas"

**Problem**: You don't have enough of the network's native token to pay transaction fees.

**Solution**: 
- Ethereum network â†’ Need ETH for gas
- Polygon network â†’ Need MATIC for gas
- BSC network â†’ Need BNB for gas

### "Transaction Failed"

**Causes**:
- Insufficient gas limit
- Slippage too low (for swaps)
- Contract error

**Solution**: Try increasing gas limit or slippage tolerance.

### "Wrong Network"

**Problem**: You're on the wrong blockchain network.

**Solution**: Switch to the correct network using the network dropdown.
